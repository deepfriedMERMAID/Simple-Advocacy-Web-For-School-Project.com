PROTECT THE FOREST

Many creatures find a natural home in the forest. This environment must be protected because it provides animals with a safe haven where they can find food, drink, and shelter while avoiding danger. Additionally, forests support a stable climate and give food to animals that live in them or rely on them for survival. There are many different species on the planet, and it is our duty to protect them. Some animals need conservation, but some are endangered and dying out. The better the planet will be, the more we can do to help these animals. It would also be a great help to us human because forests is also one of the main sources of ingredients in making medicine.

Carbon dioxide is absorbed and stored by trees. When forests are cut down or even just disturbed, carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases are released. Around 10% of global warming is caused by the destruction and loss of forests. If we don't put an end to deforestation, there is just no way that we can combat the climate issue.
Forests purify the air we breathe, filter the water we drink, prevent erosion,
and act as an important buffer against climate change.
